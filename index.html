  1. First of all, what in the hell is an ADSR?
    2. ADSR is a wave-generating method used in electronic music for some style points. Rather than a boring constant-over-time sine wave, this method generates sound waves that are more pleasing to the average listener. On top of that, it helps softwares emulate the timbre of real life instruments such as flutes and guitars. It's also present in pretty much all music composing softwares, and has become a staple in electronic music since its birth.
  2. But what does ADSR stands for?
    3. A: Attack; means how long the wave takes to go from amplitude (volume) zero to its peak;
    D: Decay; means how long the wave takes to go from its peak to its sustain volume;
    S: Sustain; means the stabilized wave volume after the peak;
    R: Release; means the amount of time spent to reach zero after the key has been relased. It's worth noting that the key can be released at any point, including during Attack and Decay phases;
